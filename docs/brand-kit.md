## 2. What we actually are (and are NOT)
**We ARE:** an end-to-end **sales** AI — the "Lead Engine." We run the whole
journey from first ad-click to a booked site visit:
**Attract → Capture → Qualify → Follow-up → Book the site visit → Warm hand-off to the sales team.**
Channels we operate: inbound voice AI, outbound calling campaigns, WhatsApp
follow-ups, a website chatbot, and social/organic lead capture (Instagram, YouTube).

**We are NOT:**
- ❌ A marketing/ads agency (we *complement* marketing; sales is the headline).
- ❌ Just a CRM or a database (we *work* the leads, not just store them).
- ❌ A generic "AI dialer" (we do the full funnel, not one channel).

**We replace:** the builder's scattered stack of *marketing agency + telecallers +
follow-up tools* with one system.

---

## 3. Who we talk to (audience)
- **Primary:** Owners, sales heads, and marketing heads at **Indian real estate
  builders / developers** (residential projects, townships, plots).
- **Context:** They spend heavily on leads but lose 30–40% to slow or no
  follow-up. Their telecallers miss calls, work 9–6, speak limited languages, and
  don't follow up at night or weekends. They care about **site visits booked**
  and **cost per booked visit**, not vanity metrics.
- **Sophistication:** Business decision-makers, often 35–60. Not developers — keep
  it plain, concrete, and outcome-focused. No heavy tech jargon.

## 4. Brand voice & tone
**Personality:** Confident, credible, calm, and plainspoken — a sharp operator, not
a hype-man. Premium but grounded. We sound like the smartest person in the room
who doesn't need to shout.

**Do:**
- Lead with the **outcome** (booked site visits, no missed leads, speed-to-lead).
- Be **specific and concrete** ("calls back in 30 seconds," "in Telugu at 11pm").
- Use **short, declarative sentences.** Confident cadence.
- Speak the customer's reality (missed calls, telecaller churn, weekend leads).
- Use real numbers when we have them; frame benefits in **rupees and site visits**.

**Don't:**
- ❌ **Never overpromise.** Do **not** claim we "convert every lead" or "guarantee"
  bookings. We help you **miss fewer leads and book *more* site visits** — relative,
  not absolute. This is a hard rule.
- ❌ No breathless hype ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "10x overnight").
- ❌ No buzzword soup ("synergy," "leverage paradigms").
- ❌ Don't promise things we don't do — e.g., we do **not** arrange driver pickup
  for site visits. After a booking, the lead gets the **sales person's details, a
  brochure, and a map** — never a promise of transport.
- ❌ Don't be robotic or corporate-stiff; don't be jokey/casual either.

**Tone dial:** Professional 8/10 · Warm 6/10 · Bold 7/10 · Playful 2/10.

**Sound like this:**
> "Your best lead came in at 11pm on a Sunday. A telecaller sees it Monday at 11am.
> By then they've booked with someone else. Kriven AI calls back in 30 seconds — in
> their language — and books the visit while you sleep."

**Not like this:**
> "Our revolutionary AI-powered synergy platform leverages next-gen automation to
> 10x your conversions and guarantee every single lead becomes a sale!"

## 5. Messaging pillars (rotate these as post themes)
1. **Speed-to-lead** — the first responder wins; seconds matter, not hours.
2. **Never miss a lead** — every channel, every hour, every language, captured.
3. **Qualify + follow-up on autopilot** — persistent, polite, tireless follow-up.
4. **Any Indian language, 24/7** — Hindi, Telugu, Tamil, Marathi, etc., round the clock.
5. **One platform vs. a scattered stack** — replaces agency + telecallers + tools.
6. **Booked site visits, not just leads** — the metric that matters to builders.
7. **The Lead Engine** — the full funnel, ad-click to site visit, as one story.

## 8. Visual identity

### Colors (exact)
| Role | Hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Brand / accent** | `#d9583e` | Terracotta-coral — the signature color. Use for CTAs, highlights, key words. |
| Brand soft | `#e87a5f` | Lighter coral for hovers/soft accents. |
| Brand tint | `#fdf1ec` | Faint coral wash for backgrounds/badges. |
| Brand gradient | `#d9583e → #b9421a` | 135° linear, for headline accents. |
| **Background / canvas** | `#faf9f6` | Warm bone / off-white — **not** stark white. |
| **Ink / text** | `#1a1817` | Warm near-black. |
| Card surface | `#ffffff` | Pure white cards on the bone canvas. |
| Muted text | `#5c5851` | Secondary copy. |
| Subtle text | `#6b6760` | Captions, meta. |
| Border | `#e6e1d8` | Warm hairlines. |
| Success | `#0a8b62` · Warning `#b45309` · Info `#1f6feb` | Status accents only. |

**The feel:** warm, premium, editorial. A **bone/cream canvas with warm near-black
ink and a single confident terracotta accent** — deliberately *not* the cold blue/
stark-white look of generic tech SaaS. Think refined, calm, expensive.

### Typography
- **Typeface:** **Geist** (geometric sans) for everything; **Geist Mono** for
  numbers/code accents.
- **Headlines:** semibold, tight letter-spacing, large and confident.
- **Body:** clean, readable, generous line-height.
- For non-web tools, closest substitutes: Inter, or system UI sans.

### Logo
- **"Kriven AI" wordmark** + a **"KV" monogram** mark (with a downward chevron/
  arrow motif) in the terracotta brand color on a light ground.
- Clearspace: keep generous padding; never crowd it.
- Backgrounds: prefer the bone canvas (`#faf9f6`) or white; the coral mark reads
  best on light. **[FILL IN: attach official logo files / SVG when using visually.]**

### Imagery / mood
- Clean product UI (dashboards), warm minimal backgrounds, subtle hand-drawn city
  skyline motif, soft depth. Avoid stocky "handshake/cityscape cliché" photos.
